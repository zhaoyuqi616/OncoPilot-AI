KRAS G12C has become a druggable target in lung adenocarcinoma. Covalent inhibitors such as sotorasib and adagrasib can produce clinical responses in selected patients. Despite this progress, intrinsic and acquired resistance remain major challenges. Resistance mechanisms may involve secondary KRAS alterations, reactivation of MAPK signaling, activation of receptor tyrosine kinases, and tumor heterogeneity. Combination therapies targeting SHP2, EGFR, or MEK are being explored to improve durability of response.
