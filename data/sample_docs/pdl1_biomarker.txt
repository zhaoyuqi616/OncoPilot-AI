Programmed death-ligand 1 (PD-L1) expression is widely used as a biomarker for immunotherapy selection in several tumor types. In NSCLC, higher PD-L1 expression may support use of checkpoint inhibitors, but responses can still occur in some patients with low PD-L1. Assay differences, temporal heterogeneity, spatial heterogeneity, and dynamic immune states complicate interpretation. PD-L1 is therefore useful but imperfect and is often considered together with clinical and molecular features.
