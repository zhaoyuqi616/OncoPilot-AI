MET amplification is a recurrent bypass mechanism of resistance in EGFR-mutant NSCLC. It can restore downstream signaling independently of EGFR inhibition and reduce the effectiveness of EGFR tyrosine kinase inhibitors. Combination strategies involving EGFR and MET inhibition have been explored to overcome this resistance state. Accurate detection may require tissue sequencing, fluorescence in situ hybridization, or plasma-based genomic profiling.
