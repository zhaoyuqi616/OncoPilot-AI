Epidermal growth factor receptor (EGFR) mutations are common oncogenic drivers in non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC). Patients with EGFR exon 19 deletion or L858R substitution often respond to EGFR tyrosine kinase inhibitors. However, acquired resistance eventually emerges. Common resistance mechanisms include EGFR T790M, MET amplification, histologic transformation, bypass signaling activation, and downstream pathway alterations. Osimertinib has improved outcomes in EGFR-mutant disease, but resistance to osimertinib can still occur through on-target and off-target mechanisms. Molecular profiling at progression is important for guiding next-line therapy.
